I finished my Software Engineering Technology program at Centennial College, packed a carry on, and moved to Germany. People keep asking why. Here is the honest version.

## The market math

In 2026 Germany has roughly one hundred and nine thousand unfilled IT positions. Around eighty percent of companies report an acute shortage of technical specialists. Software engineering is a Mangelberuf which means the EU Blue Card threshold drops to forty five thousand nine hundred and thirty four euros for IT roles. That is below the entry level salary range for Berlin, Munich, and Cologne, which means the visa pathway is unusually friendly for engineers.

Compare that to Canada in the same year. The market tightened. Junior demand softened. Senior demand stayed strong but interview loops grew longer.

## The cultural math

I wanted to learn another working culture. I already had English and French from school in Toronto and Turkish from home. German felt like the next natural language to add. I am at B1 to B2 and pushing toward C1. Working in a German team is one of the fastest ways to get there.

Berlin and Cologne run on English in tech. Munich is more bilingual. That gave me a sane runway to onboard while my German caught up.

## The boring practical steps

These took longer than I expected.

1. **Documents**. Apostille on the diploma, certified translations of school transcripts, a fresh passport with at least eighteen months on the clock.
2. **Anmeldung**. Within fourteen days of moving in, you must register your address at the Buergeramt. Without the Anmeldung confirmation you cannot open a bank account.
3. **Bank account**. N26, ING, and Commerzbank all let you onboard remotely with the Anmeldung. I went with N26 because the app is the only thing that made my first week sane.
4. **Krankenversicherung**. Health insurance is mandatory. As a new arrival looking for a job I went with private cover, then will switch to a statutory provider once employed.
5. **Steuer ID**. The tax ID arrives by post automatically a few weeks after the Anmeldung. You cannot start a job without it.

## The portfolio rewrite

Because I am suddenly applying in Germany, this site needed a real overhaul. I added an Impressum and Datenschutzerklaerung, both of which are legally required. I built a Lebenslauf alternate at /lebenslauf with the German format. I added an Anschreiben generator that drafts a German cover letter for any company you point it at. The location strings everywhere now read Germany.

Nothing about that change was hard. It just had to be done with German conventions in mind.

## What I am looking for

Full stack and platform roles. Berlin, Munich, Cologne, or remote within the EU. I am eligible for the EU Blue Card at the IT shortage threshold. Reach out if your team is hiring.
